# Analysis about the influence of ideology in judicial behaviour
This project presents a quantitative analysis of judicial decision-making at the Court of Justice of the European Union (CJEU). The primary objective is to investigate whether the political ideology of the governments that appoint CJEU judges serves as a predictor for rulings in favor of the European Commission, which is often viewed as the primary driver of European integration. 
Historically, European legal scholarship has viewed CJEU judges as "technocrats" who mechanically apply legal norms (Epstein et al., 2013, p.50). This study challenges that "legalist myth" by testing the attitudinal model of judicial behavior—which posits that policy preferences drive judicial outcomes—within the unique institutional framework of the EU.
